Ice-Creams

  • Chocolate Ice Cream

    Chocolate ice cream is a decadent and beloved dessert that you can easily make at home. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, unsweetened cocoa powder, and a pinch of salt. Whisk the mixture over medium heat until it starts to steam, but do not let it boil. Remove from heat. Add the chopped semisweet chocolate to the hot cream mixture. Let it sit for a minute to soften, then whisk until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth. Once cooled, cover the mixture with plastic wrap directly on the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Chill it in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight until thoroughly cold. Transfer the churned ice cream into a freezer-safe container. Cover tightly with a lid or plastic wrap. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours or until firm. Once frozen, scoop the chocolate ice cream into bowls or cones. You can garnish with chocolate shavings, sprinkles, or a drizzle of chocolate syrup before serving.

Cook over medium heat until the currants release their juices and become soft. This process helps extract the flavor and sweetness from the currants. In a separate bowl, whisk together heavy cream, milk, vanilla extract, and lemon juice until well combined. Add the cooled black currant mixture to the cream mixture and stir until evenly incorporated. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container, cover it with a lid or plastic wrap, and freeze for several hours or overnight until firm. Once the black currant ice cream is fully frozen, scoop it into bowls or cones. For added garnish, you can sprinkle some fresh black currants on top or drizzle with a bit of black currant syrup. Enjoy the creamy and fruity goodness of homemade black currant ice cream as a refreshing dessert or snack.

  • Pista Badam

    Pista Badam ice cream is a delicious and creamy dessert that combines the rich flavors of pistachios (pista) and almonds (badam). The ice cream base is prepared by heating a mixture of milk, cream, sugar, and sometimes condensed milk or evaporated milk. This mixture is gently simmered until the sugar is dissolved and the flavors meld together. The ice cream mixture is then churned in an ice cream maker or frozen using the traditional method of hand-churning. This process incorporates air into the mixture, giving the ice cream its creamy and smooth texture. Before serving, it is often garnished with a sprinkle of chopped pistachios and almonds for a visually appealing presentation. Pista Badam ice cream is enjoyed by people of all ages for its rich, nutty flavor, creamy texture, and delightful crunch from the toasted nuts.

  • Fruit and Nut

    Fruit and Nut ice cream is a delightful dessert that combines the sweetness of dried fruits with the crunchiness of assorted nuts. The ice cream base is prepared by heating a mixture of milk, cream, sugar, and sometimes condensed milk or egg yolks. This mixture is heated until the sugar is dissolved and the flavors are infused. Once the ice cream base is prepared and cooled, the softened dried fruits and toasted nuts are folded into the mixture. This ensures that every scoop of ice cream contains a generous amount of fruits and nuts. The mixture is then churned in an ice cream maker or frozen using the traditional method of hand-churning to create a smooth and creamy texture. After churning, the ice cream is transferred to a container and frozen until it reaches the desired firmness. Before serving, Fruit and Nut ice cream may be garnished with additional chopped nuts or a sprinkle of dried fruits for a visually appealing presentation.
